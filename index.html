Andhra Pradesh

Tucked in Andhra's Eastern Ghats, Araku Valley is a scenic escape. Lush forests, cascading waterfalls, and coffee estates create a tranquil paradise. Tribal communities, especially the Araku tribe, enrich the valley with their culture, showcased at the Tribal Museum. Reaching Araku is an adventure itself - the Visakhapatnam-Araku train route snakes through tunnels and bridges, offering stunning views. Hikers can conquer Vanjangi point for a magical sunrise, while others can indulge in swimming or rappelling. Don't miss the Borra Caves, with their captivating rock formations. Araku's pleasant weather, mesmerizing beauty, and tribal heritage make it a hidden gem for nature lovers.

Tamil Nadu

The Adiyogi Shiva statue is a colossal bust of Shiva located in Coimbatore, Tamil Nadu, India. It's part of the Isha Yoga Center complex and holds the Guinness World Record for the "Largest Bust Sculpture" in the world. Designed by Sadhguru Jaggi Vasudev, the founder of Isha Foundation, the statue towers 112 feet tall, symbolizing the 112 paths to attain enlightenment in yogic culture and the 112 chakras in the human body. Adiyogi translates to "the first yogi", signifying Shiva as the originator of yoga.

Goa

Beyond the beaches, Goa's Portuguese heritage is evident in its architecture, churches, and cuisine. The capital city, Panaji, is known for its colorful Portuguese houses and charming churches. Visitors can explore the imposing Basilica of Bom Jesus, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, and indulge in delicious Goan curries flavored with coconut and local spices. Goa also caters to adventure enthusiasts. Dolphin watching tours are a popular activity, allowing visitors to spot these playful creatures in their natural habitat.

Maharashtra

Mumbai, the "City of Dreams," is a captivating metropolis in western India. It's a vibrant blend of history, culture, and modern energy, offering something for every traveler. Immerse yourself in Mumbai's rich history at iconic landmarks like the Gateway of India, a majestic archway built to commemorate the arrival of King George V and Queen Mary in 1911. Explore the Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj Terminus (CST), a UNESCO World Heritage Site known for its stunning Victorian Gothic architecture. Mumbai also boasts a stunning coastline. Relax on the beaches of Chowpatty or Juhu, popular for their lively atmosphere and delicious street food vendors.

Bangalore

Bangalore, India's "Garden City," is a vibrant mix of modern tech hub and rich history. Explore majestic palaces like Bangalore Palace, a reflection of British colonial grandeur. For a nature fix, stroll through Cubbon Park, a sprawling haven of greenery, or visit the scenic Lalbagh Botanical Garden.Bangalore's tech focus is evident in its bustling streets and innovative energy. Visit the Indian Institute of Science, a renowned center for scientific research. Indulge in delicious South Indian cuisine, from filter coffee to masala dosa. Don't miss exploring the vibrant local markets for unique souvenirs. Bangalore offers a unique blend of history, nature, and modern charm.
